## F.1 The measurable space of world-states

- Let **╬й** be the space of **world-histories**. A point `╧Й тИИ ╬й` is one complete history of the world (a path),
  not an instantaneous snapshot тАФ this matters because records are time-indexed.
- The value space is **V = ╬г\*** тАФ finite UTF-8 strings in NFC (the ┬з5 value model). *Everything* a record carries
  is a point of `V`; numbers, times, and hashes are their string encodings. (This is why canon is
  string-only: the coordinates literally live in `╬г\*`.)
- Each partition/field is a **coordinate map** at a frame `t`:
  `Xс╡в,тВЬ : ╬й тЖТ V`.
  A partition is a measurable coordinate of the world-history тАФ "what the value of field *i* is, at frame *t*."

The world-history carries two further coordinate families, declared here so that every later ╧Г-algebra is
generated by honest random objects on ╬й (16th-round rigor pass):
- **`LтВЬ : ╬й тЖТ LogSpace`** тАФ the anchor journal at real time `t` in history `╧Й`, where **`LogSpace` is the space
  of PARTIAL MAPS `FrameID тЗА HashSpace`** (which frame slot carries which committed hash тАФ faithful to the real
  journal, which is frame-indexed: one sealed hash per slot, M4). Append-only is a property of the coordinate:
  along every path, `s тЙд t тЗТ LтВЫ(╧Й) тКС LтВЬ(╧Й)` (map extension; a substrate entry exists only at its registered
  finality, ┬з11.2 тАФ a rollbackable confirmation is not yet in the map).
- **`W_n : ╬й тЖТ AuthSpace`**, for each name `n` тАФ the name-authority observables of `n` in history `╧Й`: which
  genesis/key-log the domain serves, and what independent witnesses observed it serve (┬з12).

The **base measurable space** is `(╬й, ЁЭУХ)` where `ЁЭУХ := ╧Г( { Xс╡в,тВЬ, LтВЬ, W_n } )` is the ╧Г-algebra generated by ALL
coordinate maps; every coordinate is `ЁЭУХ`-measurable by construction, and every ╧Г-algebra below is a sub-╧Г-algebra
of `ЁЭУХ`. `LogSpace` and `AuthSpace` carry their natural **cylindrical ╧Г-algebras** (generated by the evaluation
maps `L тЖж L(frame)` and the coordinate projections of `AuthSpace`), so evaluations of the journal are measurable
functions of it. Time indices range over the protocol's DISCRETE frame grid `ЁЭХЛ` (30-second slots and their
coarsenings) тАФ continuous-time pathologies (right-continuity, usual conditions) do not arise.

A **state** at frame `t` is the tuple `(XтВБ,тВЬ, тАж, XтВЩ,тВЬ)`; a **record** (transcript) `R` is a publisher's signed
commitment to the observed values of that tuple.

## F.2 Measurement vs reality тАФ the origin of "fixation, not truth"

Distinguish two maps into `V`:

- `Yс╡в,тВЬ : ╬й тЖТ V` тАФ the **true** coordinate (what the field actually is).
- `Mс╡в,тВЬ : ╬й тЖТ V` тАФ the **measurement** the publisher recorded (a sensor reading, a computation, a copy-paste).

In general `Mс╡в,тВЬ = m(Yс╡в,тВЬ, ╬╡с╡в,тВЬ)` for some measurement map `m` and disturbance `╬╡` (a wrong sensor, a rounding,
a lie). `V = ╬г*` is a space of strings тАФ there is no addition on it; `╬╡` is an ABSTRACT deviation (the reading
differs from the truth in whatever way), not an additive noise term.

**A UST record binds `Mс╡в,тВЬ`, never `Yс╡в,тВЬ`.** It fixes *the measurement the publisher committed to, including its
error*. This is the entire content of "fixation, not truth" (┬з1): the protocol certifies `M`, and is silent on
the deviation between `M` and `Y`. Accountability follows тАФ you learn *which publisher committed which `M` at which `t`* тАФ but truth
(`Y`) is out of scope by construction.

## F.3 The anchor journal is a filtration

Let the external append-only log (the anchor substrate, ┬з11) define, for each real time `t`:

  **FтВЬ := ╧Г( Lс╡д : u тЙд t ),**  the ╧Г-algebra generated by the JOURNAL COORDINATE SUB-PROCESS up to `t` тАФ the
  family of maps `{ Lс╡д }_{u тЙд t}` from F.1, NOT the current set `LтВЬ` alone (a snapshot forgets WHEN each hash
  entered, and `╧Г` of a single set does not recover the past тАФ the two-independent-red-team fix, 17th round).
  The atomic events are `E_{h,u} := { ╧Й : h тИИ Lс╡д(╧Й) }`; "the log" is the explicit F.1 coordinate, no
  self-reference remains.

**Proposition F.3 (FтВЬ is a filtration).** `{FтВЬ}` is a filtration: each `FтВЬ` is a ╧Г-algebra, and `s тЙд t тЗТ FтВЫ тКЖ FтВЬ`.

*Proof.* Each `FтВЬ` is generated by a family of measurable maps, hence a ╧Г-algebra. For `s тЙд t` the generating
FAMILY nests literally тАФ `{ Lс╡д }_{u тЙд s} тКЖ { Lс╡д }_{u тЙд t}` тАФ and the ╧Г-algebra generated by a larger family of
maps contains the one generated by a sub-family; therefore `FтВЫ тКЖ FтВЬ`. (This is the correct move: the earlier
"`LтВЫ тКЖ LтВЬ` hence `╧Г(LтВЫ) тКЖ ╧Г(LтВЬ)`" was the classic `A тКЖ B тЗП ╧Г(A) тКЖ ╧Г(B)` trap тАФ repaired by generating from the
history `{Lс╡д}` rather than from one set.) тИО

**Corollary F.3.1 (un-backdatable time).** Backdating is the false claim that a hash `h` was present earlier
than it was. It is refuted not by a meta-statement about algebra membership but by an EVENT in the realized
history `╧Й*`: a historical checkpoint witnesses `╧Й* тИЙ E_{h,tтВА}` (h absent by `tтВА`) yet `╧Й* тИИ E_{h,tтВБ}` (present
by `tтВБ`), so the earliest provable existence is `tтВБ`, never `tтВА`. The index `t` of `{FтВЬ}` is REAL time as
attested by the anchor substrate (┬з11) тАФ the filtration is not merely ordered, it is TIME-STAMPED by an external
base the verifier
already trusts. Monotonicity alone orders events; the substrate's time base pins the order to the calendar тАФ
both together give un-backdatability, stated purely in EVENT form: the truth-values `╧Й* тИЙ E_{h,tтВА}` and
`╧Й* тИИ E_{h,tтВБ}` in the realized history (never "membership of an algebra", which is a meta-fact, not a world
event). One honest caveat (18th round): an inclusion-only Merkle log proves "existed BY `tтВБ`" but cannot by
itself prove "ABSENT at `tтВА`" тАФ refuting a backdating claim needs **authenticated non-membership** (a complete
verifiable snapshot at `tтВА`, or a non-membership proof). Without a valid `tтВА` receipt, backdating is
UNCONFIRMED; with authenticated non-membership it is REFUTED. (The reference journal is a per-hour COMPLETE
snapshot тАФ every slot of the hour listed тАФ so non-membership there is checkable by construction.) This is
exactly why a **retro-seal after an outage is fatal** тАФ against a snapshot-complete journal it is a provably
false claim about the realized history, not a mere policy breach.

## F.4 Streams are adapted processes

Model the publisher's emission as a **DocSpace-valued process** `RтВЬ : ╬й тЖТ DocSpace тИк {тКе}` (тКе = no frame at
`t`). Define the **commitment process** `HтВЬ(╧Й) := LтВЬ(╧Й)(frameтВЬ)` тАФ the journal's entry AT this frame (`HтВЬ := тКе`
where the map is undefined). Because the journal is frame-indexed (F.1) and `LogSpace` carries the cylindrical
╧Г-algebra, `HтВЬ = evalтВЬ тИШ LтВЬ` is a measurable function OF THE JOURNAL, so `╧Г(HтВЬ) тКЖ FтВЬ` holds as a theorem, not
an assertion. (Mere membership `contentHash(RтВЬ(╧Й)) тИИ range LтВЬ(╧Й)` would NOT suffice тАФ a set-valued journal
cannot single out WHICH element is this frame's hash; the frame-indexed journal is what makes the commitment
process genuinely adapted тАФ 18th-round fix.) For a verifier the useful statement is the fixed-`d` form: for a
concrete received document `d`, the ANCHORING FACT `A_{d,t} := { ╧Й : contentHash(d) тИИ range LтВЬ(╧Й) }` is an
event in `FтВЬ`. The FULL document `RтВЬ` is NOT `FтВЬ`-measurable тАФ a hash does not reveal its preimage (this is
UST's design: the anchor fixes that bytes EXISTED, it neither stores nor discloses them; ┬з11.2). `RтВЬ` is
adapted only to the richer **base world filtration** `ЁЭТвтВЬ := FтВЬ тИи ╧Г( Rс╡д : u тЙд t )`; `╧Г(RтВЬ) тКЖ ЁЭТвтВЬ` by
construction, and `ЁЭТвтВЫ тКЖ ЁЭТвтВЬ` since both components accumulate monotonically. `prev` binds `RтВЬ` to `RтВЬтВЛтВБ` inside
this filtration. **Completeness over `[tтВА, tтВБ]`** is a RANGE property (a
verdict of `verifyStream` over an interval, ┬з11.3) тАФ a single document's tier never asserts it. It is the statement that
the observed adapted process has *no missing frame* in the interval тАФ an `F_{tтВБ}`-measurable assertion witnessed by
a covering checkpoint (`M5`). "The stream is complete" = "this adapted process is fully observed up to the
stopping time `tтВБ`."

**Completeness is authenticated coverage of a committed grid тАФ no-deletion is not no-omission (M5, made precise).**
The `prev`-chain establishes a TOTAL ORDER on the observed frames and forecloses DELETION from a shown chain
(removing frame `t` orphans `t+1`'s `prev`), but it does NOT by itself foreclose OMISSION: a publisher that never
emits frame `t` and links `t+1.prev = t-1` yields a self-consistent chain WITH A HOLE. Chain-consistency and
completeness are therefore different ╧Г-algebras тАФ the former is `╧Г(prev-links)`, the latter needs the EXPECTED
index set тАФ and by Corollary F.3.1 "no frame is missing" is exactly an **authenticated non-membership** claim on
the frame axis, which does not follow from the positive `prev`-links alone. The missing coordinate is the
publisher's **cadence** `c_n(t)`, modelled as a signed, time-resolved parameter тАФ itself an adapted process,
resolved at `t` EXACTLY as the key-log resolves the active key at `t` (┬з12.2), so it cannot be shrunk post-hoc to
a coarser grid that hides slots. Then the expected grid over a closed interval is DETERMINISTIC,
`G(n,tтВА,tтВБ) := { the ust_id grid points of spacing c_n over [tтВА,tтВБ] }`, and `expected_slot_count := |G|` is a
DERIVED quantity, never stored. Define **complete over `[tтВА,tтВБ]`**: for every `g тИИ G` the observed set contains
either a frame with `ust_id = g` OR a signed gap record (┬з11.1) covering `g`, witnessed by a covering checkpoint
committing `(tтВА, tтВБ, head, cumulative_count)`. This event is `F_{tтВБ}`-measurable **only when** `c_n(t) тИИ тДР`:
without the signed cadence `|G|` is unknown, so `frame_count = |observed|` cannot be compared to `|G|`, and the
honest verdict is the strictly coarser **chain-consistent** (no-deletion), never **complete**. Note what does NOT
change: the checkpoint is the existing `class:"attestation"` with two interval bounds added to its value, the
grid is COMPUTED not stored, and the gap record already exists (┬з11.1) тАФ completeness is earned by adding the
cadence coordinate to `тДР`, not by any new document shape.

## F.5 Verification is a measurability test тАФ and the tiers are nested ╧Г-algebras

This is the load-bearing section тАФ restated in the category-correct form (16th-round rigor pass): a verifier
does NOT observe a random variable; it receives a CONCRETE document `d тИИ DocSpace` тАФ data, not chance. What IS
uncertain is the WORLD around `d`: which genesis the name really serves, what witnesses saw, what the journal
contains. So tier validity is a **parameterized predicate**: for each fixed `d`,

  `Valid_╧Д(d) : ╬й тЖТ {true, false}`,

and the tier ╧Г-algebras are generated by the WORLD-coordinates (F.1) that the tier consults тАФ never by the
document's own bytes (bytes are data; ╧Г-algebras live on ╬й):

- **ЁЭТо_LIGHT(d) := {тИЕ, ╬й}** тАФ the TRIVIAL ╧Г-algebra. `Valid_LIGHT(d)` is CONSTANT in `╧Й`: a total deterministic
  function of `d`'s bytes alone (┬з14 floor). Constancy w.r.t. the trivial algebra is the STRONGEST possible
  measurability statement тАФ this IS self-containment, said exactly.
- **ЁЭТо_HIGH(d) := ╧Г( W_n )** for the name `n` that `d` claims тАФ the name-authority coordinate (F.1): the world
  facts that bind the name to a key (genesis, key-log, witness no-fork; ┬з12).
- **ЁЭТо_TOP(d) := ╧Г( W_n ) тИи FтВЬ** тАФ the join with the filtration at the anchor time: the journal coordinate makes
  "committed by real time `t`" a world-event. (A reorg-prone substrate enters `FтВЬ` only at its registered
  finality, ┬з11.2/┬з17 тАФ nothing enters that the substrate may still roll back.)

By construction, for every fixed `d`:

  **ЁЭТо_LIGHT(d) тКЖ ЁЭТо_HIGH(d) тКЖ ЁЭТо_TOP(d).**

(The alternative modeling тАФ the document itself as a random variable `D : ╬й тЖТ DocSpace` with
`╧Г(D) тКЖ ╧Г(D,W) тКЖ ╧Г(D,W,L)` тАФ is equivalent for every theorem below and models PUBLISHER behavior; we fix `d`
because UST's verdict must be a function of the received bytes and the world's authority/journal coordinates,
never of the distribution documents are drawn from. Everywhere below, `d` is a fixed received document and
`Valid╧Д` abbreviates `Valid╧Д(d)`.)

Let a verifier's **information set** be a ╧Г-algebra `тДР` (what it can actually access: always the document; maybe a
reachable genesis/witness; maybe a reachable substrate). Let `Valid╧Д : ╬й тЖТ {true, false}` be the tier-`╧Д` validity
predicate; MEASURABILITY here always means the strict pre-image form: **the event `{╧Й тИИ ╬й : Valid╧Д(╧Й) = true}`
belongs to `ЁЭТо_╧Д`** (equivalently, `Valid╧Д` is measurable w.r.t. `ЁЭТо_╧Д` and the discrete ╧Г-algebra on `{true,
false}` тАФ stated in pre-image form because any map into a two-point space is trivially "measurable" if the
domain algebra is rich enough; the CONTENT is which algebra contains the truth-event).
predicate.

**Theorem F.5 (Tier = measurability level).**
1. **(Self-containment of the floor.)** `Valid_LIGHT` is `ЁЭТо_LIGHT`-measurable: it is a deterministic function of
   the document bytes alone (recompute canon + per-partition + `content_hash`; strict-Ed25519-verify the
   signature). Hence `ЁЭТо_LIGHT тКЖ тДР` **always** тАФ the floor is decidable offline by anyone.
2. **(Each tier strictly refines with external information.)** `Valid_HIGH` is `ЁЭТо_HIGH`-measurable but not in
   general `ЁЭТо_LIGHT`-measurable тАФ deciding it requires `ЁЭТй` (name authority). `Valid_TOP` is `ЁЭТо_TOP`-measurable
   but not in general `ЁЭТо_HIGH`-measurable тАФ it requires `FтВЬ` (the anchor).
3. **(A verdict carries the finest decidable tier.)** The reported verdict is `VALID:╧Д` where `╧Д` is the finest
   tier with `ЁЭТо_╧Д тКЖ тДР` and `Valid╧Д = true`. A bare `VALID` is never emitted (a verdict without its ╧Г-algebra is
   meaningless).
4. **(Invariant I4 as totality + determinism.)** `Valid╧Д` is defined by ┬з14 as a TOTAL, DETERMINISTIC function
   of its `ЁЭТо_╧Д`-measurable inputs тАФ total because the ┬з14a obligations table leaves no member present-but-
   unchecked and no input outside the table's domain, deterministic because every obligation is a recomputation
   with one right answer. Two verifiers given the same `ЁЭТо_╧Д` inputs therefore compute the same value тАФ not by
   assumption but because they evaluate the same total function. (This is not circular: the CONTENT of the claim
   is the totality and determinism of ┬з14/┬з14a; a verifier that secretly consults information outside `ЁЭТо_╧Д`, or
   skips an obligation, is non-conforming тАФ the theorem is exactly what conformance testing checks.)
5. **(INDETERMINATE as a missing ╧Г-algebra тАФ or a declined evaluation.)** If `ЁЭТо_╧Д(d) тКД тДР` (e.g. the witness is
   unreachable, so the name-authority coordinate `W_n тКД тДР`), then `тДР` is **in general insufficient** to decide
   `Valid_╧Д(d)` тАФ there is NO general decision procedure at tier `╧Д`. (For a PARTICULAR `d` the predicate may

   missing information forecloses the general procedure, not necessarily every instance.) The same
   outcome also covers a verifier that HOLDS the inputs but does not evaluate the function (an optional
   algorithm it does not implement, a resource budget): formally the information is present and the verifier
   declines to compute тАФ the honest report is still `INDETERMINATE(reason)`, never a guessed verdict. Distinct
   from `INVALID` in both cases. Authority is **denied, never forged**
   (`W1`): an adversary who suppresses the witness removes `ЁЭТй` from *everyone's* `тДР`, which can only *lower* a
   tier, never fabricate one.

*Sketch.* (1) canon/hash/verify are total deterministic functions of the FIXED received document `d`'s bytes;
`Valid_LIGHT(d)` is therefore constant in `╧Й`, i.e. `{тИЕ,╬й}`-measurable тАФ the strongest measurability.
(The determinism premise is NORMATIVELY guaranteed, not assumed: ┬з6 pins RFC 8785 JCS with tightenings, ┬з5's
string-only value model makes float/NaN edge cases UNREPRESENTABLE by construction, and the raw-bytes boundary

`ЁЭТо_LIGHT тКЖ ЁЭТо_HIGH тКЖ ЁЭТо_TOP` and the definition of `тДР`. тИО

**Reading of the theorem.** *"Verify" is not "is this true" тАФ it is "with respect to which ╧Г-algebra is this
record measurable, and is it measurable-true there."* The tier ladder is literally a tower of nested information

## F.5.0 Assurance is a PRODUCT LATTICE; the tier is one policy projection (#78, gaps 1тАУ3)

**M1.1 (rc.36) тАФ STRENGTH is separate from SUPPORT.** The rc.35 statement was self-contradictory: EvidenceBasis was

256` тАФ a capability set is a Boolean lattice `(P(Caps), тКЖ)`, not a 4-element chain (and the realized code axis was a
4-chain the live verifier pinned to `opaque` тАФ a phantom coordinate). The corrected structure:


Verification measures FOUR orthogonal STRENGTH coordinates, each its own information sub-╧Г-algebra of `тДР`:

**CapabilitySupport** тАФ which capabilities the ADMITTED evidence supplies тАФ is deliberately NOT a fifth strength

## F.5a No-fork is authenticated non-membership тАФ `corroborated тКК authoritative`

## F.5b Downgrade resistance is the consumer's floor, not the producer's promise

The tiers are totally ordered, `LIGHT < HIGH < TOP`, and by Theorem F.5 each is a coarser ╧Г-algebra than the
next: `ЁЭТо_LIGHT тКЖ ЁЭТо_HIGH тКЖ ЁЭТо_TOP`. Write `T(d, тДР)` for the FINEST tier decidable for document `d` from an
information set `тДР` тАФ the verdict the reference verifier emits. Three facts pin its behaviour under an attacker
who can only DELETE evidence (strip the anchor `proof`, omit the genesis / key-log, hide the name-map):

- **Monotone erosion.** `тДР' тКЖ тДР тЗТ T(d, тДР') тЙд T(d, тДР)`. Every tier above LIGHT is earned by a coordinate that
  lives OUTSIDE the document bytes тАФ the resolved key-log for HIGH (┬зF.5), the anchored map + substrate root for
  TOP (┬зF.5a, ┬зF.3). Removing any such coordinate from `тДР` removes it from the ╧Г-algebra that decides the higher
  tier, so the decidable tier can only fall. Stripping is a coarsening, never a refinement.
- **No upward forge (`W1`).** `T(d, тДР)` is never ABOVE the true tier the evidence supports: authority can be
  DENIED but not fabricated (F.3.1 / F.5a тАФ non-membership and anchored time are not producible by the
  publisher). Evidence is necessary, not assertable; there is no header, flag, or self-claim in `ЁЭТо_LIGHT` that
  raises `T`.
- **Consumer floor.** A consumer names a REQUIRED tier `R` and accepts `d` iff `T(d, тДР) тЙе R`. This is the only
  place the ordering is USED: the floor is a comparison, not a coercion.

**Theorem F.5b (downgrade resistance).** Let a consumer hold floor `R` and let an attacker present `d` with an
eroded `тДР' тКЖ тДР`. Then either `T(d, тДР') тЙе R` (the surviving evidence still earns `R` тАФ a genuine `R`-grade
document, nothing was gained) or `T(d, тДР') < R`, in which case the consumer REJECTS. There is no third branch:
because acceptance is `T тЙе R` and never "accept at whatever `T` came out," a strip that drops the tier below the
floor produces a rejection, never a silent accept at the lower tier. *Proof.* Immediate from monotone erosion
(the strip can only lower `T`) and the floor being a total-order comparison against a fixed `R`. Downgrade is the
floor doing its job; it is the consumer's CHOICE of `R`, never a forge succeeding (`W1` blocks the only other
route, raising `T`). тИО

**Corollary F.5b (the two floors are symmetric).** `requireAuthoritative` is the floor `R = HIGH`-authoritative;
`requireAnchored` is the floor `R = TOP`. A verifier MUST implement each as `T(d, тДР) тЙе R ? accept : reject`. The
rejection NAMES the missing coordinate тАФ a stripped/absent anchor proof тЗТ `E-ANCHOR` (a structural downgrade: the
document cannot reach TOP without a new proof), a non-authoritative identity тЗТ `E-GENESIS`, and an anchor that is
PRESENT and inclusion-valid but whose substrate is unreachable or not-yet-buried тЗТ `INDETERMINATE` (retry, `W1`:
the evidence may still arrive тАФ this is unavailability, not a forgery). Absence of a floor is the LIGHT default,
where `T` is surfaced as-is; the floors exist precisely so a TOP-needing consumer cannot be handed a LIGHT doc.

## F.5c Fork-choice тАФ anchor-inclusion is the choice function

A single time coordinate `ust_id` may have SEVERAL candidate documents `{dтВБ, тАж, dтВЩ}` with DISTINCT
`content_hash`es: the honest dual-writer race (main and failover both seal the slot, different arrival тЗТ
different bytes), or an adversary offering two states for one slot. A consumer holding two of them must resolve
which is canonical WITHOUT trusting local arrival order тАФ otherwise two consumers disagree, and "valid" stops
being a function of the data.

The resolvent is already in the model. The anchor journal `FтВЬ` (┬зF.3) commits, per authority and per hour, a SET
of leaves тАФ the hour's Merkle root, substrate-timestamped тАФ a single totally-ordered, tamper-evident object. Define
the choice function

  `canonical(ust_id) = the unique dс╡в with content_hash(dс╡в) тИИ leaves(A_{auth}(hour(ust_id)))`,

where `A_{auth}(h)` is the anchored leaf-set of authority `auth` for hour `h`. Its well-definedness is a counting
statement about the operator's admission rule:

- **Election тЗТ uniqueness.** The store-NX election (a content-addressed conditional-write: the first writer to
  claim `ust_id` wins, ┬з11 dual-writer) admits AT MOST ONE writer's document to the set that is later anchored.
  So in honest operation `|{ i : ch(dс╡в) тИИ A_{auth} }| тЙд 1`.
- **Exactly one anchored тЗТ that one is canonical.** The others are non-canonical LOSERS тАФ not invalid (each may
  be a perfectly `VALID:HIGH` document; it simply lost the race and was never anchored for this slot). A consumer
  deterministically keeps the anchor-included one.
- **Zero anchored тЗТ `INDETERMINATE` at TOP.** No candidate is in `FтВЬ` yet (the hour is still open, or neither was
  elected). Fork-choice is undecidable at TOP; the consumer resolves at HIGH by other means or WAITS for the hour
  anchor. This is unavailability (`W1`), not a fault.
- **Two or more anchored under ONE authority тЗТ `E-PREV` (equivocation).** `|{ i : ch(dс╡в) тИИ A_{auth} }| тЙе 2` with
  distinct `content_hash`es means the operator committed TWO states for one slot into an anchored root under one
  name тАФ a non-repudiable, detectable fault. The anchor makes it PUNISHABLE: the operator SIGNED a root
  containing both, so equivocation is evidence, not deniable ambiguity. (Distinct authorities publishing the same
  `ust_id` are NOT a fork тАФ canonicity is per-authority; each is canonical in its own `A_{auth}`.)

**Proposition F.5c (determinism).** Two consumers with the same candidate set and the same anchor `A_{auth}` emit
the SAME `canonical`. *Proof.* The choice reads only `content_hash(dс╡в)` (a function of the bytes) and membership

is a function of `(candidates, A_{auth})` alone. тИО This is exactly the property ┬з11 requires of the dual-writer:

## F.5d Key-log freshness is the third face of authenticated non-membership

The revocation predicate "key `k` is still valid at time `t`" is `┬мтИГ e : e revokes k тИз e тИИ keylog тИз time(e) ткп t`
тАФ a NON-MEMBERSHIP statement over the key-log, structurally identical to no-fork on the name axis (F.5a) and
snapshot-completeness on the time axis (F.3.1). By Corollary F.3.1 it does NOT follow from any positive view: a
consumer holding a cached key-log `L' тКЖ L` decides only `revoke тИЙ L'` (membership in its own prefix), which is in
`╧Г(L')`, NOT in the ╧Г-algebra that decides `revoke тИЙ L`. The publisher (or a stale cache) can OMIT the revoking
entry exactly as it can omit a rival genesis. So freshness is earned by bringing the non-membership coordinate
into `тДР`. **An earlier claim тАФ that an anchored key-log HEAD alone settles `┬мтИГ later entry` and thus earns
`attested` тАФ was UNSOUND (P0-03, external audit): an anchored head proves membership AT its anchor time, not that
it is the LATEST head at the target's time; a revoke that FOLLOWS the anchored prefix is invisible to it (the F.5a
"positive lookup settles ┬мтИГ" transposition fails here because the key-log has no independent map coordinate, only a
prefix).** Strong key-log freshness is therefore not a single-anchor fact: `corroborated`/`attested` are earned ONLY
through the checkpoint derivation (F.5i/F.5j) тАФ authorization тИз strict terminality (F.5n) тИз proven-after ordering тИз
independent uniqueness. A fetch from the authoritative surface timestamped `тЙе t` still earns `fresh` (a single-view
report); neither тЗТ `unverified`. Emitting `unverified` (never a forged "valid") and letting the consumer floor on it
(`requireFreshKeylog`) is the F.5b discipline: the strong word is earned by the composed predicate, not bought by
assuming a cached prefix is the whole log.

## F.5e The key-authority process `K_n(t)` тАФ a state machine, not a set (MATH-04, #75)

`W_n` (┬зF.5, ┬зF.5a) bundled several facts; one of them тАФ WHICH key is authorized for name `n` at time `t` тАФ is
not a static set but a **process**. Model the key-log as a sequence of events `(e_1, тАж, e_m)`, each an
`add | rotate | revoke` transition, and define the reducer

  `K_n : (event prefix) тЖж тЯиactive тКЖ Keys, bind тКЖ Keys, revoked : Keys тЗА {retired, compromised}├ЧTimeтЯй`,

with `active` the keys that may sign the NEXT event, `bind` every key ever authorized (for document binding), and
the transitions: `add(k)` тЗТ `active тИк {k}`, `bind тИк {k}`; `rotate` (signed by `s`, naming successor `k`) тЗТ
`active тИк {k} тИЦ {s}`, `bind тИк {k}`, `revoked[s] = (retired, t)`; `revoke(k, r)` тЗТ `active тИЦ {k}`, `revoked[k] =
(r, t)`. The **admissibility invariant** is `signer(e_{i+1}) тИИ active(after e_i)` тАФ an event is well-formed only
if its signer was active in the state the PREVIOUS events produced. This is exactly the missing coordinate: "key
`k` appears somewhere in the log" (`k тИИ bind`) is strictly weaker than "`k` was active when it signed" (`k тИИ
active` at that prefix), and conflating them is the P0-02 class (a revoked / rotated-out key still signing).

**Realization (representation note).** `K_n` is realized by `resolveKeys(genesis, keylog)` тЖТ `{active, validKeys
(=bind), revoked, history, head}`; the invariant is the `active.get(keyId(sig.pub)) === sig.pub` gate per entry;
the closed per-`op` schema is the well-formedness of each event. Document authority reads `bind` (continuity) then
the ┬з12.2 X1 predicate judges `revoked[k]` at the document's time тАФ a query of `K_n` at `t`. **The query time `t`
is the PROVEN anchor upper bound `U` (ROOT 1 / MATH-05, done): `verify` runs in two phases тАФ it verifies the anchor
FIRST, then resolves authority with that proven `U`, so revocation / retirement / freshness are decided against
the chain, not a caller-supplied or absent time.** `K_n(t)` is a WINDOW, `authorized_at(k) тЙд U тЙд end(k)`: the
lower bound is the new `premature` verdict (a document cannot be proven-anchored before its signing key was
authorized), the upper bound is the X1 retired/compromised predicate. The `authority-at-time` vectors exercise the
window; the `keylog-state` vectors exercise the reducer invariant тАФ both are executable instances a second
implementation runs through its own `K_n` and must match. (The `authorized_at` lower bound uses the key's CLAIMED
authorization time; making it an ANCHORED lower bound is the operator manifest, ROOT 3.)

## F.5f Composite authority is TRANSITIVE тАФ the impersonation fix needs no new signed object (#75 ROOT 3)

An external audit proposed closing the composition holes (an impostor becoming `canonical` / `complete` under a
victim's name, P0-03; off-grid commitment grinding, P0-04) with a new **operator-signed HourManifest**. The math
says that is unnecessary, and shows *why* тАФ resolving two apparent dilemmas ("sign the hour root or not?", "typed
leaf or raw hash?") by direct computation rather than taste.

conflating them was the whole bug. No new object closes a ╧Г-algebra gap that `K_A` already decides.

**Proposition F.5f.2 (the hour root carries no information a signature could add).** Let the hour's leaves be the

**Corollary F.5f.3 (the typed leaf is redundant).** `content_hash(d) = H("ust:state", canon({ust, state}))` and

separately. The audit's manifest collapses, by the math, to "per-frame `K_A` + `A`-signed cadence + the existing
substrate anchor" тАФ less machinery, each omission proven, not cut.

## F.5h The authority-checkpoint chain is a well-founded ADAPTED authority process (non-circular latest-head, #76/#77)

The `latest-head` fact (P0-05: which key-log head is current for a name at sequence `n`) is F.5a/F.5d authenticated
non-membership. Establishing it needs a checkpoint whose own AUTHORITY does not depend on the head it asserts тАФ
else the naive "the head `HтВЩ` signs the checkpoint that says `HтВЩ` is current" is circular. F.5h formalizes the chain
that carries checkpoint-signing authority non-circularly.

**Three layers тАФ the "sign your own signature" fixpoint is unexpressible.** A checkpoint is a body `b`, a preimage
`╧А(b) = canon({purpose:"ust:authority-checkpoint-signature", b})`, a transcript `(b, sig)`, and an identifier
`id = H("ust:authority-checkpoint", canon({b, sig}))`. Since `sig` signs `╧А(b)` and `╧А` EXCLUDES `sig`, no transcript
can commit its own signature (F.2: a document cannot fix its own hash). And `id` is a function of `(b, sig)` ALONE,
so external evidence (anchor receipts, map proofs) is outside `id`: one checkpoint under two different anchors has
ONE `id` тАФ immutable protocol state, distinct from evidence ABOUT it.

**Authority is `ЁЭУЧ_{n-1}`-adapted тАФ the checkpoint chain carries its OWN filtration (M4.1).** The index `n` is a
checkpoint SEQUENCE, not real time, so the checkpoint process is adapted to its own filtration, NOT to the real-time
anchor filtration `ЁЭУХтВЬ` (the rc.35 round-2 correction: the two orderings were conflated): `ЁЭУЧтВЛтВБ :=
╧Г(VerifiedAuthorityContext)` (genesis-verified scope + rooted authority, M2), `ЁЭУЧтВЩ := ╧Г(ЁЭУЧтВЛтВБ, CтВА,тАж,CтВЩ, admitted
recovery/transition evidence up to n)`. Define `Auth(0) =` the context's checkpoint authority (or a pinned prior's
committed next); `Auth(n) =` the key `CтВЩтВЛтВБ` committed for `n` (its `next_*` with `effective_sequence = n`), else
`Auth(n-1)`. The signer of `CтВЩ` is REQUIRED to equal `Auth(n)`, and `Auth(n)` is a function of strictly-earlier
VERIFIED state only тАФ `ЁЭУЧ_{n-1}`-measurable. `CтВЩ`'s own body cannot set `Auth(n)`: its declared next is
`effective_sequence = n+1`, authorizing only `CтВЩтВКтВБ`. So the authority relation is WELL-FOUNDED тАФ `Auth(n) тЙ║ CтВЩ`
in causal order, no cycle тАФ which is precisely the F.4-style adaptedness the naive self-authorizing head violated.
Real time enters ONLY where a checkpoint meets `ЁЭУХтВЬ` through evidence (F.5i's `ProvenAfter`), never through `n`.

**Resolve-before-trust; the carried field is redundant.** Verification computes `Auth(n)` from prior state, THEN
checks `sig` against it; the body's `current_key_id` is a diagnostic that MUST equal `Auth(n)` and NEVER resolves
it. A forged `current_key_id` therefore cannot move authority (checked, not used) тАФ the F.5a.1 "never self-declared"
rule on the authority coordinate. Rotation is a deterministic total function (all-or-none; `keyId(next_pub) =
next_key_id`; effective at `n+1`), so `Auth` is a deterministic function of the chain: two verifiers agree (F.5c).
Absent a root for `Auth(0)` (no genesis authority, no pinned prior) the process is unresolved тЗТ
`INDETERMINATE(authority_unresolved)`, NEVER a fallback to `CтВЩ`'s carried key.

*(F.5h is the AUTHORIZATION backbone; the `corroborated`-freshness derivation on top тАФ terminality + consistency +
external commitment + `proven-after` the target, F.5d ├Ч F.5g тАФ is Phase B proper, the next increment.)*

**Realization.** `buildAuthorityCheckpoint` (body), `sealAuthorityCheckpoint` (`╧А`-preimage sign),

## F.5l Checkpoint recovery тАФ a genesis-rooted threshold re-adapts the authority process without breaking well-foundedness (#76 ┬з1.7)

## F.5n Strict key-log terminality тАФ head is the LAST entry, not merely a member (#77)

F.5i's `corroborated` needs the checkpoint head to be TERMINAL: the latest key-log entry, with no more-recent
revoking successor. Bare membership `head тИИ root` is NOT terminality тАФ a log that ALSO holds a successor still
contains `head`, so membership is satisfied while the head is stale.

**An earlier construction was UNSOUND (P0-02, external audit).** Committing the key-log as a positioned SMT keyed by
`H(index)` and proving `Inclusion(L-1) тИз NonMembership(L)` proves only that index `L` is empty тАФ it says NOTHING
about indices `L+1, L+2, тАж`. Because hashed-index leaves are scattered, `[L, тИЮ)` is not a subtree, so a
single-coordinate non-membership cannot cover the suffix: a prover commits entries at `{0, 2}`, claims `length = 1`,
proves `pos 1` empty, and hides the entry at `pos 2`. Sparse-dictionary absence at one coordinate is not
prefix-contiguity тАФ the claim "non-membership at `L` proves nothing follows" was false.

**Terminality is a SNAPSHOT property of a SIZE-BOUND vector commitment (M4.3).** The key-log is an ORDERED Merkle
over EXACTLY `L` leaves (padded to a power of two with a domain-separated empty leaf), and the committed root binds
the size: `root = H("ust:keylog-commit", {length = L, merkle_root})`. `SnapshotTerminal(root, L, head)` holds iff
WITHIN the fixed committed root of width `next-pow2(L)`: `head` is the leaf at index `L-1`; every RIGHT sibling on
its authentication path (each time the path node is a left child) is the empty-subtree default for its level; the
proof depth is EXACTLY `ceil(log2(width))` with the index fully consumed (the P0-5 refinement тАФ an under-depth proof
recomputes a smaller tree); and the recomputed root equals the committed root. This proves the admissible index
domain of THIS SNAPSHOT is exactly `[0, L-1]` тАФ no committed leaf exists at or beyond `L` *in this root*. It is
deliberately NOT a claim about "the suffix `[L, тИЮ)` forever" (the rc.35 round-2 over-strength): nothing about a
FUTURE, larger snapshot follows from one commitment тАФ that relation is `ChainConsistent` (F.5i/M4.2, append-only
ACROSS snapshots), and currency against real time is `ProvenAfter` (F.5i). Freshness is the COMPOSITION of a
snapshot-terminal, chain-consistent, committed, proven-after, authorized checkpoint тАФ three orthogonal predicates,
none overloaded. Still `SnapshotTerminal тКЛ HeadInRoot`, and тАФ unlike the SMT version тАФ sound against a successor at
ANY index of the committed tree, adjacent or not.

**Realization.** `buildKeylogCommitment(entryHashes)` (ordered size-bound Merkle: `root`/`length`/`head`/`merkle_root`
/`headProof = {index, siblings}` + a `prove(index)`) and `verifyKeylogTerminality({root, length, head}, {headProof})`
(index `= L-1` тИз every right sibling on the path is that level's empty default тИз recomputed
`H("ust:keylog-commit", {length, merkle_root})` equals `root`), composed into `deriveCheckpointFreshness` as the
`Terminal` conjunct (F.5i).

**Conformance (math тЗТ code тЗТ green vector, `packages/ust-protocol/conformance.mjs`).**
- terminality holds for honest logs: *"TERM honest length-1 log (head at pos0, nothing at pos1) тЖТ terminal"*, *"TERM honest length-2 log (head at pos1, nothing at pos2) тЖТ terminal"*.
- a hidden/truncated successor is caught: *"TERM strict catches a HIDDEN SUCCESSOR (length lies)"* тАФ now via the P0-5 proof-depth check (siblings тЙа ceil(log2(width))) as well as the right-subtree-empty test.
- head must sit at the terminal index: *"TERM wrong head at position L-1 тЖТ not terminal"*.

Sound against the P0-02 reproduction (`security-regression.mjs`: a real length-3 log presented as length-1 тЗТ not terminal).

## F.6 Composition тАФ the event algebra

An **anchored existence-and-commitment claim** is an event `A тИИ FтВЬ`; an UNANCHORED signed claim is a document
predicate and enters `FтВЬ` only upon anchoring (a LIGHT document's statements exist before any journal sees
them). Because each `FтВЬ` is a ╧Г-algebra, anchored claims are closed under `тИй` (AND), `тИк` (OR), and `с╢Ь` (NOT).
The ┬з9 combinators are exactly this closure:

- **Attestation** over constituents `cтВБ,тАж,cтВЩ` commits to the conjunction `A = AтВБ тИй тАж тИй AтВЩ` where each `Aс╡в` is
  the EXISTENCE-and-commitment event of record `i` тАФ never its truth, and (per ┬з14a) the attestation alone does
  not even verify the constituents' own signatures; it fixes the SET. The Merkle `root` is the binding of that
  set; walking into the constituents is the referent walk (depth-budgeted, reported).
- **Derivation** has TWO strengths that must not be conflated. `based_on` alone is a **declared** lineage тАФ
  `DeclaredDerivation(d, H)`, the publisher's signed CLAIM that `d` draws on hashes `H`; it does not prove any
  particular function was applied. Only when a verifier holds a deterministic profile `╧Ж`, the source documents,
  and recomputes тАФ `VerifiedDerivation_╧Ж(d, H)` тАФ is `d = ╧Ж(H)` measurable, and only THEN does composition of
  measurable maps propagate provenance measurability along the DAG (`E-CYCLE`
  guarantees `╧Ж` is well-founded / acyclic).

So "combine" never manufactures information тАФ it only takes measurable functions of already-measurable events.

## F.7 The certainty predicate тАФ and where probability is (and isn't) load-bearing

For a committed record with event `A тИИ FтВЬ`, no probability measure is needed to state the certainty claim тАФ
it is a MEASURABILITY statement (formally: under ANY measure, e.g. a point mass on the realized history,
`E[1_A | FтВЬ] = 1_A`; we do not rely on one):

  **1_A is FтВЬ-measurable, and on the event `A` itself `1_A = 1` тАФ i.e. `1_A(╧Й*) = 1` for a realized history `╧Й*`
  whose inclusion proof checks (not globally on ╬й, where histories without the commitment give `1_A = 0`).**

That is the precise content of the informal `P(record(╧Й,t) | FтВЬ) = 1`: *given the filtration at `t`, whether the record was
committed is already known* (measurable) and, for a valid sealed record, certain. **In pure UST every verdict is
deterministic тАФ `0/1` тАФ so the probability is degenerate.** The genuinely load-bearing structure is the
**filtered measurable space `(╬й, F, {FтВЬ})` plus a deterministic measurability predicate**, not a probability
measure. Rigor demands saying so plainly: UST has no randomness of its own.

Probability becomes non-degenerate at **exactly one boundary** тАФ the measurement gap of ┬зF.2. Modelling
the deviation `╬╡` (with `M = m(Y, ╬╡)`, ┬зF.2 тАФ no subtraction exists on strings) as a random variable is where a
real (non-`0/1`) measure lives. UST deliberately does **not** model it:
it fixes `M` (with `╬╡` baked in) and stops. So the honest scoping is:

- **filtration тЗТ verification** (deterministic, the heart of UST);
- **probability тЗТ measurement error only** (downstream of UST, in whatever application reasons about how far a
  publisher's measurement `M` might deviate from the truth `Y`).

Conflating the two тАФ reading `P(...) = 1` as if UST asserted something probabilistic about *reality* тАФ is the
formal shape of the "signed = true" over-read the whole protocol exists to forbid.

## F.7a Scope note тАФ private layers

Private partitions and layer chains (┬з10/┬з10a) are REPRESENTED here only through their commitments: a blinded
or encrypted layer contributes its commitment event to `FтВЬ` exactly like a public one (existence and time are
public facts), while its VALUE remains outside every `тДР` that lacks the disclosure. Selective disclosure is,

## F.7b ╬й, concretely (instantiation note)

╬й is the space of complete world-histories: each ╧Й is one full assignment of timestamped events тАФ physical
readings, computations, publications. The model needs only a MEASURABLE space (╧Г-algebras of information),
never a probability measure (F.7): the realized history is a single ╧Й, and the filtration `F_t` is generated
by the anchor journal тАФ a PARTIAL observation of ╧Й up to `t`. UST records fix the publisher's MEASUREMENT `M`
of coordinates of ╧Й, never the true `Y` (F.2). Implementations never instantiate ╬й in code; it exists so that

interpretation of ╬й but because they evaluate the same TOTAL function of the same record (I4) тАФ ╬й-independence
is the point, not a gap.

## F.9 Resource-bounded verification (non-normative; the numbers stay normative in ┬з13)

Information is not the only condition of decidability: a verifier also operates under a finite resource
budget. Alongside its information set `тДР_v`, give every verifier a budget `╧Б_v = (M_v, T_v, S_v, N_v)` тАФ
memory, time, safe stack depth, and the provenance-node allowance. A verifier lives in the PAIR `(тДР_v, ╧Б_v)`.
For a transcript `R` define the size vector

  `╬╝(R) = (B, P, D, A, F, W, K)`

тАФ canonical UTF-8 bytes of the signed content, partition count, nesting depth, max array length, direct
reference breadth, per-call walk volume, and the examined key-log epoch length. Let
`C_v : DocSpace ├Ч Request тЖТ тДЭтБ┤тВК` be the resource-cost vector of parsing, canonicalising, hashing, signature
verification and the requested walk тАФ components `(memory bytes, time, stack frames, visited nodes)` for
verifier `v`'s implementation тАФ with `╧Б_v тИИ тДЭтБ┤тВК` and `ткп` the COMPONENTWISE partial order (`a ткп b тЗФ aс╡в тЙд bс╡в`
for all four); "insufficient resources" means SOME component exceeds its budget. A tier predicate is decidable
by `v` only when BOTH hold: `ЁЭТо_╧Д тКЖ тДР_v` (information) and `C_v(R) ткп ╧Б_v` (computation). Missing information
yields `INDETERMINATE(unavailable)`; insufficient local resources yield `INDETERMINATE(resource_limit)`
(the wire reasons of ┬з15). Neither makes a protocol-valid transcript INVALID.

### F.9.1 Extensive and structural metrics

A metric is a **VOLUME** metric when legitimate publisher data increases it extensively (тЙИ additively under
union), growth leaves the verification CONTROL STRUCTURE unchanged (more of the same work, not different
work), and excess payload admits a commitment-preserving externalization (content-address the bulk, keep the
signed hash). `B` and `P` are VOLUME metrics; per-partition ciphertext bytes are volume too but NOT an
independent coordinate of `╬╝_V` тАФ they are already included in `B(R)`, and any separate per-partition ciphertext
cap is an admission rule outside `╬║` (classification and vector stay aligned, 18th round). A metric is a **STRUCTURE**
metric when increasing it enlarges recursion, branching, traversal or resolution state for EVERY verifier
regardless of publisher authority, and a protocol-native transformation can express the same data without the
increase. `D, A, F, W, K` are STRUCTURE metrics. Hence the law:

  **Authority may enlarge legitimate volume, but cannot make structure cheaper to verify.**

That is why VOLUME bounds are ceremony-declarable and STRUCTURE bounds are protocol-wide laws (┬з13).

### F.9.2 Capacity earned by authority

Let `╬║тВА = (BтВА, PтВА)` be the universal LIGHT floor, `╬║_ABS = (B_ABS, P_ABS)` the protocol ceiling, and let an
authoritative genesis declare `╬║_G` subject to `╬║тВА ткп ╬║_G ткп ╬║_ABS` (an out-of-range declaration is not a valid
declaration тАФ the floor applies). The declaration is EFFECTIVE only when its genesis binding lies in the
verifier's authority information: `G_╬║ тИИ ЁЭТЬ` and `ЁЭТЬ тКЖ тДР_v`. Effective capacity:

  `╬║(R, тДР_v) = ╬║_G` if the authoritative genesis is verified and in range; `╬║тВА` otherwise.

A self-signed declaration does not enlarge capacity because key possession alone never places it in the
name-authority ╧Г-algebra: `ЁЭТЬ тКД тДТ`. On the wire this is rc.12's TRUSTED GRANT: the grant flows FROM
`resolveAuthority` (or the caller's pin/policy), never from a caller-attached genesis document.

### F.9.3 Bounded-verification verdict

With `S_ABS` the vector of absolute STRUCTURE bounds:

  `V(R; тДР_v, ╧Б_v) =`
  `INVALID(E-BOUNDS)` if `╬╝_S(R) тЛа S_ABS` тАФ structure over the absolute law;
  `INVALID(E-BOUNDS)` if `╬╝_V(R) тЛа ╬║_ABS` тАФ volume over the absolute protocol ceiling (checked BEFORE authority:
    a 1 GiB transcript is not-a-UST regardless of any grant тАФ the reference impl already tests `sBytes > ABS`
    first, rc.12; the model now matches it);
  `INDETERMINATE(unavailable)` if `╬╝_V(R) тЛа ╬║тВА тИз ╬╝_V(R) ткп ╬║_ABS` and no trusted grant is in `тДР_v` тАФ evidence missing;
  `INVALID(E-BOUNDS)` if `╬╝_V(R) тЛа ╬║(R, тДР_v)` тАФ over the granted capacity;
  `INDETERMINATE(resource_limit)` if `C_v(R) тЛа ╧Б_v` тАФ beyond THIS verifier;
  `Valid_╧Д(R)` otherwise.

Protocol invalidity (structure OR absolute volume), missing authority evidence and insufficient verifier
resources are distinct states.
Transport admission (refusing an over-budget RAW input before decoding) is a fourth, pre-verdict state: a
refusal to start, reported as `resource_limit`, never a statement about the document.

### F.9.4 Portability of the LIGHT floor

Let `ЁЭУФтВА` be the declared class of baseline environments and `╧БтВА = inf_{eтИИЁЭУФтВА} ╧Б_e`. The floor is PORTABLE
exactly when **every environment meets ITS OWN budget**: `тИА e тИИ ЁЭУФтВА : sup { C_e(R) : ╬╝_V(R) ткп ╬║тВА, ╬╝_S(R) ткп S_ABS }
ткп ╧Б_e` (the crossed form `sup_e C_e ткп inf_e ╧Б_e` is sufficient but NOT necessary тАФ a heavy-but-well-budgeted
environment and a light-but-tight one can each pass their own condition while failing the crossed one тАФ 18th-round fix) тАФ every transcript inside the floor is both
information-decidable from its own bytes and computationally decidable by every baseline implementation. This
is the resource form of LIGHT self-containment.

### F.9.5 Structural escape transformations

An absolute STRUCTURE bound does not reduce expressive power when a protocol-native, commitment-preserving
transformation exists: `4096 = 64┬▓` (a two-level attestation tree carries the full constituent universe at
breadth 64); oversized arrays chunk; deep data restructures; long key histories split into re-genesis epochs;
long walks continue across bounded calls. Each transformation preserves the existence and hash-binding of the
represented state while keeping every single verification inside a universal resource envelope.

### F.9.6 Calibration of the numerical constants

The model derives the INEQUALITIES the constants must satisfy; it cannot make one assignment uniquely
necessary. Concrete values are calibration parameters with explicit, falsifiable premises:

- `F_ABS = тМИтИЪP_ABSтМЙ = тМИтИЪ4096тМЙ = 64` тАФ the minimal uniform branching factor representing the full partition
  universe in a two-level tree (the two-level requirement is the declared design premise; 64 then FOLLOWS).
- `A_ABS = P_ABS = 4096` тАФ one canonical operation can hold the full universe, no slack.
- `B_ABS = ╬╖ ┬╖ P_ABS ┬╖ s╠Д = 4 ├Ч 4096 ├Ч 4 KiB = 64 MiB` тАФ declared premises: target partition payload
  `s╠Д = 4 KiB`, encoding-and-safety factor `╬╖ = 4`. Falsifiable by vectors and memory benchmarks.
- `BтВА = 1 MiB` тАФ the power-of-two floor of `min` over the baseline environment class ЁЭУФтВА (browser, agent tool,
  CLI, message/file transport, serverless) of each environment's transport cap, `(M_e тИТ r_e)/╬▒_e` and

**The model derives the law; benchmarks calibrate the constants.** While `╬й` and `FтВЬ` are purely mathematical
objects, the resource bounds `╧Б_v` and the numeric constants (`BтВА`, `╬║`, тАж) are ENGINEERING PARAMETERS

through explicitly published premises. A change in the target environment class or the measured amplification
requires recalibration тАФ not a reinterpretation of the mathematics.

### One-paragraph summary

Model the world as a filtered measurable space `(╬й, F, {FтВЬ})` whose filtration **is** the append-only anchor
journal. Fields are coordinate maps; a record fixes the publisher's *measurement* of them, never the truth. To
**verify** is to test **measurability**: `VALID:LIGHT/HIGH/TOP` names the FINEST of three nested ╧Г-algebras (the
highest tier reached)
(`ЁЭТо_LIGHT тКЖ ЁЭТо_HIGH тКЖ ЁЭТо_TOP`) with respect to which the record is measurable-and-true тАФ the floor from the
document alone, HIGH adding the name-authority ╧Г-algebra, TOP adding the filtration itself. Two conforming
verifiers agree because the verdict is a single-valued measurable function (I4); a verifier lacking the needed
╧Г-algebra returns INDETERMINATE, never INVALID. Probability is degenerate in the core and load-bearing only across
the measurement-vs-reality gap тАФ which is precisely the line UST refuses to cross.
