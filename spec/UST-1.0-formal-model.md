## F.2 Measurement vs reality ‚Äî the origin of "fixation, not truth"

Distinguish two maps into `V`:

- `Y·µ¢,‚Çú : Œ© ‚Üí V` ‚Äî the **true** coordinate (what the field actually is).
- `M·µ¢,‚Çú : Œ© ‚Üí V` ‚Äî the **measurement** the publisher recorded (a sensor reading, a computation, a copy-paste).

In general `M·µ¢,‚Çú = m(Y·µ¢,‚Çú, Œµ·µ¢,‚Çú)` for some measurement map `m` and disturbance `Œµ` (a wrong sensor, a rounding,
a lie). `V = Œ£*` is a space of strings ‚Äî there is no addition on it; `Œµ` is an ABSTRACT deviation (the reading
differs from the truth in whatever way), not an additive noise term.

**A UST record binds `M·µ¢,‚Çú`, never `Y·µ¢,‚Çú`.** It fixes *the measurement the publisher committed to, including its
error*. This is the entire content of "fixation, not truth" (¬ß1): the protocol certifies `M`, and is silent on
the deviation between `M` and `Y`. Accountability follows ‚Äî you learn *which publisher committed which `M` at which `t`* ‚Äî but truth
(`Y`) is out of scope by construction.

both together give un-backdatability, stated purely in EVENT form: the truth-values `œâ* ‚àâ E_{h,t‚ÇÄ}` and
`œâ* ‚àà E_{h,t‚ÇÅ}` in the realized history (never "membership of an algebra", which is a meta-fact, not a world

## F.4 Streams are adapted processes

Model the publisher's emission as a **DocSpace-valued process** `R‚Çú : Œ© ‚Üí DocSpace ‚à™ {‚ä•}` (‚ä• = no frame at

**Completeness is authenticated coverage of a committed grid ‚Äî no-deletion is not no-omission (M5, made precise).**

resolved at `t` EXACTLY as the key-log resolves the active key at `t` (¬ß12.2), so it cannot be shrunk post-hoc to
a coarser grid that hides slots. Then the expected grid over a closed interval is DETERMINISTIC,
`G(n,t‚ÇÄ,t‚ÇÅ) := { the ust_id grid points of spacing c_n over [t‚ÇÄ,t‚ÇÅ] }`, and `expected_slot_count := |G|` is a

cadence coordinate to `‚Ñê`, not by any new document shape.

## F.5b Downgrade resistance is the consumer's floor, not the producer's promise

The tiers are totally ordered, `LIGHT < HIGH < TOP`, and by Theorem F.5 each is a coarser œÉ-algebra than the
next: `ùíÆ_LIGHT ‚äÜ ùíÆ_HIGH ‚äÜ ùíÆ_TOP`. Write `T(d, ‚Ñê)` for the FINEST tier decidable for document `d` from an
information set `‚Ñê` ‚Äî the verdict the reference verifier emits. Three facts pin its behaviour under an attacker
who can only DELETE evidence (strip the anchor `proof`, omit the genesis / key-log, hide the name-map):

- **Monotone erosion.** `‚Ñê' ‚äÜ ‚Ñê ‚áí T(d, ‚Ñê') ‚â§ T(d, ‚Ñê)`. Every tier above LIGHT is earned by a coordinate that
  lives OUTSIDE the document bytes ‚Äî the resolved key-log for HIGH (¬ßF.5), the anchored map + substrate root for
  TOP (¬ßF.5a, ¬ßF.3). Removing any such coordinate from `‚Ñê` removes it from the œÉ-algebra that decides the higher
  tier, so the decidable tier can only fall. Stripping is a coarsening, never a refinement.
- **No upward forge (`W1`).** `T(d, ‚Ñê)` is never ABOVE the true tier the evidence supports: authority can be
  DENIED but not fabricated (F.3.1 / F.5a ‚Äî non-membership and anchored time are not producible by the
  publisher). Evidence is necessary, not assertable; there is no header, flag, or self-claim in `ùíÆ_LIGHT` that
  raises `T`.
- **Consumer floor.** A consumer names a REQUIRED tier `R` and accepts `d` iff `T(d, ‚Ñê) ‚â• R`. This is the only
  place the ordering is USED: the floor is a comparison, not a coercion.

**Theorem F.5b (downgrade resistance).** Let a consumer hold floor `R` and let an attacker present `d` with an
eroded `‚Ñê' ‚äÜ ‚Ñê`. Then either `T(d, ‚Ñê') ‚â• R` (the surviving evidence still earns `R` ‚Äî a genuine `R`-grade
document, nothing was gained) or `T(d, ‚Ñê') < R`, in which case the consumer REJECTS. There is no third branch:
because acceptance is `T ‚â• R` and never "accept at whatever `T` came out," a strip that drops the tier below the
floor produces a rejection, never a silent accept at the lower tier. *Proof.* Immediate from monotone erosion
(the strip can only lower `T`) and the floor being a total-order comparison against a fixed `R`. Downgrade is the
floor doing its job; it is the consumer's CHOICE of `R`, never a forge succeeding (`W1` blocks the only other
route, raising `T`). ‚àé

**Corollary F.5b (the two floors are symmetric).** `requireAuthoritative` is the floor `R = HIGH`-authoritative;
`requireAnchored` is the floor `R = TOP`. A verifier MUST implement each as `T(d, ‚Ñê) ‚â• R ? accept : reject`. The
rejection NAMES the missing coordinate ‚Äî a stripped/absent anchor proof ‚áí `E-ANCHOR` (a structural downgrade: the
document cannot reach TOP without a new proof), a non-authoritative identity ‚áí `E-GENESIS`, and an anchor that is
PRESENT and inclusion-valid but whose substrate is unreachable or not-yet-buried ‚áí `INDETERMINATE` (retry, `W1`:
the evidence may still arrive ‚Äî this is unavailability, not a forgery). Absence of a floor is the LIGHT default,
where `T` is surfaced as-is; the floors exist precisely so a TOP-needing consumer cannot be handed a LIGHT doc.

statement about the operator's admission rule:

- **Election ‚áí uniqueness.** The store-NX election (a content-addressed conditional-write: the first writer to
  claim `ust_id` wins, ¬ß11 dual-writer) admits AT MOST ONE writer's document to the set that is later anchored.
  So in honest operation `|{ i : ch(d·µ¢) ‚àà A_{auth} }| ‚â§ 1`.
- **Exactly one anchored ‚áí that one is canonical.** The others are non-canonical LOSERS ‚Äî not invalid (each may
  be a perfectly `VALID:HIGH` document; it simply lost the race and was never anchored for this slot). A consumer
  deterministically keeps the anchor-included one.
- **Zero anchored ‚áí `INDETERMINATE` at TOP.** No candidate is in `F‚Çú` yet (the hour is still open, or neither was
  elected). Fork-choice is undecidable at TOP; the consumer resolves at HIGH by other means or WAITS for the hour
  anchor. This is unavailability (`W1`), not a fault.
- **Two or more anchored under ONE authority ‚áí `E-PREV` (equivocation).** `|{ i : ch(d·µ¢) ‚àà A_{auth} }| ‚â• 2` with
  distinct `content_hash`es means the operator committed TWO states for one slot into an anchored root under one
  name ‚Äî a non-repudiable, detectable fault. The anchor makes it PUNISHABLE: the operator SIGNED a root
  containing both, so equivocation is evidence, not deniable ambiguity. (Distinct authorities publishing the same
  `ust_id` are NOT a fork ‚Äî canonicity is per-authority; each is canonical in its own `A_{auth}`.)

**Proposition F.5c (determinism).** Two consumers with the same candidate set and the same anchor `A_{auth}` emit
the SAME `canonical`. *Proof.* The choice reads only `content_hash(d·µ¢)` (a function of the bytes) and membership
in `A_{auth}` (a function of the shared anchor); neither depends on arrival order or local state. So `canonical`
is a function of `(candidates, A_{auth})` alone. ‚àé This is exactly the property ¬ß11 requires of the dual-writer:
"canonical = anchor-included" turns an operator-side race into a consumer-side FUNCTION ‚Äî the loser is decided by
the chain, not by whichever document a given agent happened to fetch first.

## F.5d Key-log freshness is the third face of authenticated non-membership

The revocation predicate "key `k` is still valid at time `t`" is `¬¨‚àÉ e : e revokes k ‚àß e ‚àà keylog ‚àß time(e) ‚™Ø t`
‚Äî a NON-MEMBERSHIP statement over the key-log, structurally identical to no-fork on the name axis (F.5a) and
snapshot-completeness on the time axis (F.3.1). By Corollary F.3.1 it does NOT follow from any positive view: a
consumer holding a cached key-log `L' ‚äÜ L` decides only `revoke ‚àâ L'` (membership in its own prefix), which is in
`œÉ(L')`, NOT in the œÉ-algebra that decides `revoke ‚àâ L`. The publisher (or a stale cache) can OMIT the revoking
entry exactly as it can omit a rival genesis. So freshness is earned by bringing the non-membership coordinate
into `‚Ñê`. **An earlier claim ‚Äî that an anchored key-log HEAD alone settles `¬¨‚àÉ later entry` and thus earns
`attested` ‚Äî was UNSOUND (P0-03, external audit): an anchored head proves membership AT its anchor time, not that
it is the LATEST head at the target's time; a revoke that FOLLOWS the anchored prefix is invisible to it (the F.5a
"positive lookup settles ¬¨‚àÉ" transposition fails here because the key-log has no independent map coordinate, only a
prefix).** Strong key-log freshness is therefore not a single-anchor fact: `corroborated`/`attested` are earned ONLY
through the checkpoint derivation (F.5i/F.5j) ‚Äî authorization ‚àß strict terminality (F.5n) ‚àß proven-after ordering ‚àß
independent uniqueness. A fetch from the authoritative surface timestamped `‚â• t` still earns `fresh` (a single-view
report); neither ‚áí `unverified`. Emitting `unverified` (never a forged "valid") and letting the consumer floor on it
(`requireFreshKeylog`) is the F.5b discipline: the strong word is earned by the composed predicate, not bought by
assuming a cached prefix is the whole log.

## F.5e The key-authority process `K_n(t)` ‚Äî a state machine, not a set (MATH-04, #75)

`W_n` (¬ßF.5, ¬ßF.5a) bundled several facts; one of them ‚Äî WHICH key is authorized for name `n` at time `t` ‚Äî is
not a static set but a **process**. Model the key-log as a sequence of events `(e_1, ‚Ä¶, e_m)`, each an
`add | rotate | revoke` transition, and define the reducer

  `K_n : (event prefix) ‚Ü¶ ‚ü®active ‚äÜ Keys, bind ‚äÜ Keys, revoked : Keys ‚áÄ {retired, compromised}√óTime‚ü©`,

with `active` the keys that may sign the NEXT event, `bind` every key ever authorized (for document binding), and
the transitions: `add(k)` ‚áí `active ‚à™ {k}`, `bind ‚à™ {k}`; `rotate` (signed by `s`, naming successor `k`) ‚áí
`active ‚à™ {k} ‚àñ {s}`, `bind ‚à™ {k}`, `revoked[s] = (retired, t)`; `revoke(k, r)` ‚áí `active ‚àñ {k}`, `revoked[k] =
(r, t)`. The **admissibility invariant** is `signer(e_{i+1}) ‚àà active(after e_i)` ‚Äî an event is well-formed only
if its signer was active in the state the PREVIOUS events produced. This is exactly the missing coordinate: "key
`k` appears somewhere in the log" (`k ‚àà bind`) is strictly weaker than "`k` was active when it signed" (`k ‚àà
active` at that prefix), and conflating them is the P0-02 class (a revoked / rotated-out key still signing).

**Realization (representation note).** `K_n` is realized by `resolveKeys(genesis, keylog)` ‚Üí `{active, validKeys
(=bind), revoked, history, head}`; the invariant is the `active.get(keyId(sig.pub)) === sig.pub` gate per entry;
the closed per-`op` schema is the well-formedness of each event. Document authority reads `bind` (continuity) then
the ¬ß12.2 X1 predicate judges `revoked[k]` at the document's time ‚Äî a query of `K_n` at `t`. **The query time `t`
is the PROVEN anchor upper bound `U` (ROOT 1 / MATH-05, done): `verify` runs in two phases ‚Äî it verifies the anchor
FIRST, then resolves authority with that proven `U`, so revocation / retirement / freshness are decided against
the chain, not a caller-supplied or absent time.** `K_n(t)` is a WINDOW, `authorized_at(k) ‚â§ U ‚â§ end(k)`: the
lower bound is the new `premature` verdict (a document cannot be proven-anchored before its signing key was
authorized), the upper bound is the X1 retired/compromised predicate. The `authority-at-time` vectors exercise the
window; the `keylog-state` vectors exercise the reducer invariant ‚Äî both are executable instances a second
implementation runs through its own `K_n` and must match. (The `authorized_at` lower bound uses the key's CLAIMED
authorization time; making it an ANCHORED lower bound is the operator manifest, ROOT 3.)

separately. The audit's manifest collapses, by the math, to "per-frame `K_A` + `A`-signed cadence + the existing

## F.5l Checkpoint recovery ‚Äî a genesis-rooted threshold re-adapts the authority process without breaking well-foundedness (#76 ¬ß1.7)

F.5h's authority process halts if the currently-authorized checkpoint key is lost: `Auth(n)` was to be committed by
`C‚Çô‚Çã‚ÇÅ`, and no one can now produce it. F.5l adds a SECOND, genesis-rooted authority source that re-adapts the
process ‚Äî without self-authorization and without bypassing any other checkpoint predicate.

**A second `ùìï_{n-1}`-measurable authority.** Genesis fixes a recovery key set `RK` (role-separated from data and
checkpoint keys, immutable within the epoch) and a threshold `t`. A recovery statement for sequence `n` is authorized
iff `‚â• t` DISTINCT keys of `RK` sign the BYTE-IDENTICAL typed claim binding `(domain, genesis_epoch, last_accepted =
id(C‚Çô‚Çã‚ÇÅ), effective_sequence = n, replacement_authority)`. The recovered authority `Auth_rec(n)` is then the claim's
`replacement_authority`. Both candidate authorities are measurable in the past: normal `Auth(n)` from `C‚Çô‚Çã‚ÇÅ`, and
`Auth_rec(n)` from `RK ‚äÜ ùìï_0` (genesis) plus a statement bound to `id(C‚Çô‚Çã‚ÇÅ)`. Neither reads `C‚Çô`'s own content, so
`Auth_rec(n) ‚â∫ C‚Çô` ‚Äî the F.4/F.5h adaptedness and well-foundedness hold. Recovery is NOT self-authorization: the LOST
key does not sign; the genesis threshold does. The verifier accepts `C‚Çô` iff its signature matches `Auth(n)` OR
`Auth_rec(n)`, resolved before the signature is trusted (F.5h resolve-before-trust, extended to a two-element set).

**Threshold authorizes ONE replacement but does NOT prove no rival ‚Äî the conflict must be DETECTED.** An earlier
claim ‚Äî that two statements naming different replacements are "each below `t`, so neither recovers" ‚Äî was FALSE
(P0-05, external audit): with OVERLAPPING quorums a single EQUIVOCATING recovery signer makes two conflicting

tolerates the LOSS of `|RK|‚àít` keys; it does NOT guarantee a unique decision under a Byzantine signer. So the
verifier GROUPS distinct valid signers by the canonical claim they signed and REJECTS ‚Äî a detectable authority
conflict, never an array-order-dependent pick ‚Äî if MORE THAN ONE distinct replacement reaches `t`; it also requires
`1 ‚â§ t ‚â§ |RK|` (`t = 0` is not authorization). Threshold is formal AUTHORIZATION of one replacement; the independent
rule that SELECTS it is UNIQUE threshold-attainment, exactly as F.5j/F.5k separate authorization from anti-equivocation.

**Bounded and non-bypassing.** `effective_sequence = n` binds recovery to EXACTLY the next checkpoint; it
re-authorizes the SIGNER coordinate only. `C‚Çô` still passes every other predicate ‚Äî sequence, `previous_checkpoint`
linkage, rotation exactness, terminality, consistency, external commitment, and (for `attested`) independent
uniqueness. So recovery is a re-rooting WITHIN the F.5h chain, not an escape from it; and because `RK` is
genesis-fixed and role-separated, a normal checkpoint key can never silently replace the dormant emergency roots.

## F.5n Strict key-log terminality ‚Äî head is the LAST entry, not merely a member (#77)

F.5i's `corroborated` needs the checkpoint head to be TERMINAL: the latest key-log entry, with no more-recent
revoking successor. Bare membership `head ‚àà root` is NOT terminality ‚Äî a log that ALSO holds a successor still
contains `head`, so membership is satisfied while the head is stale.

**An earlier construction was UNSOUND (P0-02, external audit).** Committing the key-log as a positioned SMT keyed by
`H(index)` and proving `Inclusion(L-1) ‚àß NonMembership(L)` proves only that index `L` is empty ‚Äî it says NOTHING
about indices `L+1, L+2, ‚Ä¶`. Because hashed-index leaves are scattered, `[L, ‚àû)` is not a subtree, so a
single-coordinate non-membership cannot cover the suffix: a prover commits entries at `{0, 2}`, claims `length = 1`,
proves `pos 1` empty, and hides the entry at `pos 2`. Sparse-dictionary absence at one coordinate is not
prefix-contiguity ‚Äî the claim "non-membership at `L` proves nothing follows" was false.

**Terminality is a SNAPSHOT property of a SIZE-BOUND vector commitment (M4.3).** The key-log is an ORDERED Merkle
over EXACTLY `L` leaves (padded to a power of two with a domain-separated empty leaf), and the committed root binds
the size: `root = H("ust:keylog-commit", {length = L, merkle_root})`. `SnapshotTerminal(root, L, head)` holds iff
WITHIN the fixed committed root of width `next-pow2(L)`: `head` is the leaf at index `L-1`; every RIGHT sibling on
its authentication path (each time the path node is a left child) is the empty-subtree default for its level; the
proof depth is EXACTLY `ceil(log2(width))` with the index fully consumed (the P0-5 refinement ‚Äî an under-depth proof
recomputes a smaller tree); and the recomputed root equals the committed root. This proves the admissible index
domain of THIS SNAPSHOT is exactly `[0, L-1]` ‚Äî no committed leaf exists at or beyond `L` *in this root*. It is
deliberately NOT a claim about "the suffix `[L, ‚àû)` forever" (the rc.35 round-2 over-strength): nothing about a
FUTURE, larger snapshot follows from one commitment ‚Äî that relation is `ChainConsistent` (F.5i/M4.2, append-only
ACROSS snapshots), and currency against real time is `ProvenAfter` (F.5i). Freshness is the COMPOSITION of a
snapshot-terminal, chain-consistent, committed, proven-after, authorized checkpoint ‚Äî three orthogonal predicates,
none overloaded. Still `SnapshotTerminal ‚äã HeadInRoot`, and ‚Äî unlike the SMT version ‚Äî sound against a successor at
ANY index of the committed tree, adjacent or not.

**Realization.** `buildKeylogCommitment(entryHashes)` (ordered size-bound Merkle: `root`/`length`/`head`/`merkle_root`

## F.7 The certainty predicate ‚Äî and where probability is (and isn't) load-bearing

For a committed record with event `A ‚àà F‚Çú`, no probability measure is needed to state the certainty claim ‚Äî
it is a MEASURABILITY statement (formally: under ANY measure, e.g. a point mass on the realized history,
`E[1_A | F‚Çú] = 1_A`; we do not rely on one):

  **1_A is F‚Çú-measurable, and on the event `A` itself `1_A = 1` ‚Äî i.e. `1_A(œâ*) = 1` for a realized history `œâ*`
  whose inclusion proof checks (not globally on Œ©, where histories without the commitment give `1_A = 0`).**

That is the precise content of the informal `P(record(œâ,t) | F‚Çú) = 1`: *given the filtration at `t`, whether the record was
committed is already known* (measurable) and, for a valid sealed record, certain. **In pure UST every verdict is
deterministic ‚Äî `0/1` ‚Äî so the probability is degenerate.** The genuinely load-bearing structure is the
**filtered measurable space `(Œ©, F, {F‚Çú})` plus a deterministic measurability predicate**, not a probability
measure. Rigor demands saying so plainly: UST has no randomness of its own.

Probability becomes non-degenerate at **exactly one boundary** ‚Äî the measurement gap of ¬ßF.2. Modelling
the deviation `Œµ` (with `M = m(Y, Œµ)`, ¬ßF.2 ‚Äî no subtraction exists on strings) as a random variable is where a
real (non-`0/1`) measure lives. UST deliberately does **not** model it:
it fixes `M` (with `Œµ` baked in) and stops. So the honest scoping is:

- **filtration ‚áí verification** (deterministic, the heart of UST);
- **probability ‚áí measurement error only** (downstream of UST, in whatever application reasons about how far a
  publisher's measurement `M` might deviate from the truth `Y`).

Conflating the two ‚Äî reading `P(...) = 1` as if UST asserted something probabilistic about *reality* ‚Äî is the
formal shape of the "signed = true" over-read the whole protocol exists to forbid.

### F.9.1 Extensive and structural metrics

A metric is a **VOLUME** metric when legitimate publisher data increases it extensively (‚âà additively under

work), and excess payload admits a commitment-preserving externalization (content-address the bulk, keep the

independent coordinate of `Œº_V` ‚Äî they are already included in `B(R)`, and any separate per-partition ciphertext

  **Authority may enlarge legitimate volume, but cannot make structure cheaper to verify.**
